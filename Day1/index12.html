About

The Eiffel Tower, locally nicknamed "La Dame de Fer" (Iron Lady), is located on the Champ de Mars in Paris’s 7th arrondissement at 5 Avenue Anatole France, near the Seine River.
It was designed and constructed by Gustave Eiffel’s engineering company between 1887 and 1889 as the centerpiece of the 1889 Exposition Universelle, commemorating the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution.
Initially criticized for its radical design, it has become a global symbol of France and one of the most recognizable structures in the world.
